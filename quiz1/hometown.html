Pekanbaru

Kota Bertuah

Get To Know

Pekanbaru is the capital city of Riau province in Indonesia, located on the island of Sumatra. Known for its thriving oil industry, it serves as a major economic hub, particularly in petroleum and palm oil production. The city is culturally rich, with a blend of Malay and Minangkabau influences, reflected in its architecture, traditions, and cuisine. Pekanbaru also acts as a gateway to nearby natural attractions like the Siak River and the Riau Archipelago. Despite its industrial growth, the city is also focused on developing infrastructure and tourism.

Tourism Place

Pekanbaru, though known for its industry, offers several attractions for visitors. The stunning An-Nur Grand Mosque and the culturally rich Sultan Syarif Kasim II Museum are popular spots. The scenic Siak River provides beautiful views, while Taman Wisata Alam Mayang offers outdoor activities for families. As a gateway to the Riau Archipelago, Pekanbaru has more to explore.

Delicious Food

Pekanbaru's food scene is a blend of Malay and Minangkabau influences. Popular dishes include Sate Danguang-Danguang, a savory beef satay, and Gulai Ikan Patin, a rich fish curry. For snacks, try Kue Bangkit and Lempuk Durian, a sweet durian-based treat. With its unique flavors, Pekanbaru offers a culinary experience worth exploring.
