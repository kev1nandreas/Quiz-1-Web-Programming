Tourism Place in Pekanbaru

Masjid Raya An Nur

Masjid Raya An-Nur is a grand mosque located in Pekanbaru, Riau, often referred to as the "Taj Mahal of Sumatra" due to its majestic architecture. Built in 1968, the mosque showcases a blend of Malay, Turkish, and Indian architectural styles, with a large central dome and spacious prayer halls. It serves as both a religious and cultural landmark, attracting visitors for its beauty and spiritual significance.

Alam Mayang

Alam Mayang is a popular recreational park in Pekanbaru, Riau, offering outdoor activities for families and visitors. Known for its fishing ponds, the park also features paddle boats, playgrounds, and scenic picnic areas. Surrounded by greenery, it's a great spot for relaxation and enjoying nature, making it a favorite weekend getaway for locals.

Soeman HS Library

Soeman HS Library is a prominent public library in Pekanbaru, Riau, named after the famous Indonesian writer Soeman HS. Known for its unique, modern architecture resembling a traditional bookstand, the library serves as a cultural and educational hub.
